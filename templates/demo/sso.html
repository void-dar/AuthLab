What is SSO?

Single Sign-On lets users authenticate once with a central Identity Provider (IdP) and then access multiple services without logging in again. Examples: signing into your company Google account gives access to Gmail, Drive, Meet, etc.

Standards: SAML 2.0 (enterprise), OpenID Connect (modern web), Kerberos (Windows/Active Directory)

🆚 SSO Benefits

Without SSO

  • Separate password for each app
  • Password fatigue → weak passwords
  • Each app manages credentials
  • Offboarding requires N revocations
  • Higher breach surface area

With SSO

  • One strong password + MFA
  • Central authentication enforcement
  • Instant revocation on offboarding
  • Unified audit log
  • Better security hygiene
